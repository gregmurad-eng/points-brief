Lead Story

The Hyatt clock starts now: lock in 1:1 before October

The best-kept transfer ratio in your wallet has an expiration date. Chase has confirmed that existing Sapphire Preferred and Ink Business Preferred cardholders will keep the cherished 1:1 transfer from Ultimate Rewards to World of Hyatt only through September 30, 2026. From October 1, that rate steps down to 4:3 — meaning 1,000 Chase points will buy 750 Hyatt points instead of 1,000. Anyone who applied for either card on or after June 15 is already on the 4:3 rate.

For your stack, this is the single most consequential change of the year. Hyatt remains the rare hotel program where points routinely return north of 2 cents apiece, and the 1:1 pipeline from Chase is what makes it sing. There is no need to speculatively transfer — Chase points are worth more sitting flexible than parked in Hyatt — but if you have a confirmed redemption in mind for late 2026 or 2027, the math says move those points before the September 30 cliff.
