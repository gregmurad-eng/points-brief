Lead Story

The Sapphire Preferred Refresh Lands — and Hyatt Transfers Take the Hit

The Chase Sapphire Preferred refresh became effective yesterday, June 15, and it’s a genuinely mixed bag for points people. On the plus side: new 3X categories on gas and EV charging plus vacation rentals like Airbnb and Vrbo, a doubled annual hotel credit (now $100), and a new $120 Global Entry / TSA PreCheck / NEXUS credit every four years — all for the same unchanged $95 annual fee.

But there’s a sting that matters for this audience: Ultimate Rewards transfers to World of Hyatt are being devalued to a 4:3 ratio for Sapphire Preferred cardholders, and the old 10% anniversary point bonus is gone. Hyatt has long been the crown jewel of the UR transfer chart, so if you hold the Preferred and lean on Hyatt redemptions, this is the line to read twice. Sapphire Reserve holders keep the 1:1 Hyatt transfer — worth weighing if you straddle both cards. Full breakdown at Frequent Miler.

Today’s Briefs

United MileagePlus

Buy miles with up to a 100% bonus — clock runs to June 24

United’s buy-miles sale offers up to a 100% bonus (some accounts see 55%), dropping the effective cost to roughly 1.88¢ per mile if you’re targeted for the top tier. The catch: a hard 50,000-mile purchase cap this round, down sharply from the old 200k ceiling. Only worth it against a specific premium-cabin redemption. Details.

Delta SkyMiles

Quiet Delta One flash sale to Europe this summer

Delta is running an unadvertised flash sale on Delta One business class to Europe, best availability to Italy, Spain, France and the UK between June and August. One-way pricing starts around 115k miles, and a Delta cardholder’s 15% discount can bring it down near 97,750 SkyMiles each way. Search before it tightens.

World of Hyatt

Up to 30,000 bonus points on summer stays

Register and earn 3,000 bonus points per qualifying stay at participating Hyatt Place and Hyatt Select hotels across the Americas, June 1 through July 31, up to 30,000 total. Stays must be three consecutive nights to count. Free points on stays you may already have booked. Register first.

Sapphire Reserve

New $250 hotel credit now covers IHG properties

The refreshed Sapphire Reserve carries a $250 credit for bookings at seven hotel collections including IHG, Montage and Pendry through Chase Travel. If you already hold the Reserve, this is found money against stays you’d book anyway — and it softens the case for keeping Hyatt transfers on the Preserve side. How it works.

Today’s Move

If you’ve got a Marriott redemption in sight, move Chase points now. The 55% Bonvoy bonus is the single richest transfer on either of your programs this month, and it dies June 30 alongside the Amex Flying Blue and Bonvoy bonuses. Transfers are one-way and irreversible, so only push points you have a concrete booking for — but if that booking exists, 1:1.55 is the best math on the board today.
