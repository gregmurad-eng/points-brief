Lead Story

Amex Has Quietly Stopped Running Transfer Bonuses

Through five months of 2026, Amex hasn't offered a single meaningful transfer bonus. That's a break from years of pattern — and it matters for how you think about your MR balance.

Amex Membership Rewards has historically been one of the most active programs for transfer bonuses — running promotions to partners like Avianca, Aeroplan, British Airways, and Hilton on a rolling basis. In 2026, those have all but stopped.

No Avianca bonus. No Aeroplan bonus. No meaningful airline transfer promotions at all through May. The Amex→Avios and Amex→Hilton bonuses that ran briefly in mid-May appear to have been outliers, not a sign of resumed activity.

What this means practically: if your MR strategy has relied on waiting for a bonus before transferring, that strategy is getting expensive. The longer you hold MR hoping for a bonus that may not come, the more value you're leaving on the table relative to cards with active bonus programs (Chase, Capital One, Citi).

The better approach right now: treat MR as a currency that transfers at base rate. Evaluate partners on base value, not bonus potential. Amex's best base-rate transfers remain Aeroplan (1:1), Flying Blue (1:1), and ANA (1:1) for premium cabin redemptions.

Chase → Marriott 55% — Best Redemption Math

The 55% bonus through June 30 makes Marriott more interesting than usual. The sweet spot is Category 4–5 properties at 35K–50K points standard rate — with the bonus, you're effectively getting those at a 35% discount vs. base rate. Run the numbers against cash rates before transferring.
