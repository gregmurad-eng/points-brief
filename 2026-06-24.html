Lead StoryHyatt's New Five-Tier Chart Settles In — And the Sweet Spots Have Shifted

World of Hyatt's most consequential award-chart overhaul in years is now fully in effect. As of May 20, the program retired its Off-Peak / Standard / Peak structure in favor of five pricing tiers — Lowest, Low, Moderate, Upper, and Top — and the early returns confirm what loyalists feared: the chart moved up far more than it moved down. Roughly 112 hotels climbed into a more expensive award category while only 24 dropped, according to coverage from Frequent Miler and The Points Guy.

At the top end the damage is real. A "Top" Category 8 night now runs 75,000 points, up from 45,000 — a 67% jump that erodes the program's marquee aspirational redemptions. Most properties land in the gentler middle, where shifts range from a roughly 14% decrease to a 38% increase depending on the hotel. The redeeming feature: Hyatt kept a published chart while peers drift toward opaque dynamic pricing, so you can still calculate value before you book. Bookings locked in before the cutover are protected at the old rate even if the property has since moved up. The practical takeaway for the weeks ahead is to re-run the math on every Hyatt redemption you had penciled in — the standout sweet spots are now in the Low and Moderate tiers, not at the top.

In BriefToday's Briefs

UnitedStatus-match window closes June 30

Elites from Delta, Alaska, American and other carriers have until June 30 to register for 120 days of complimentary United MileagePlus status. If you carry mid-tier status elsewhere and have United flying on the calendar, this is a near-free upgrade to better seats and boarding. Details on United's What's New page.

UnitedBasic economy now earns zero miles

Since April 2, most basic-economy bookings earn no MileagePlus miles unless you hold United status or a co-branded card. Meanwhile, PlusPoints and complimentary Premier upgrades are now eligible on award tickets, and card spend counts toward PQP — a quietly meaningful shift for how status gets earned this year. (NerdWallet)

DeltaSkyMiles holds the line for 2026

In a rare bout of restraint, Delta is keeping Medallion qualifying thresholds and MQD requirements unchanged for 2026, with only modest tweaks to Choice Benefits. After several years of tightening, a flat year reads as loyalty-friendly. (Business Traveller)

AmexNew 30% bonus to Virgin Atlantic

Amex just launched a fresh 30% transfer bonus to Virgin Atlantic Flying Club, live through July 31. Flying Club remains a quietly excellent way into Delta One, ANA first, and domestic partner awards — and at 30% off, the buy-in is unusually cheap. (AwardWallet)

Today's Move

If you hold elite status with Delta, Alaska, or American and have any United travel ahead, register for the United status match before June 30 — 120 days of complimentary MileagePlus status is the single highest-return, zero-points action on the board this week. Then, only if you have a confirmed Virgin Atlantic award in mind, lean on the 30% Amex bonus running through July 31 rather than speculatively moving points for the June 30 Chase-to-Marriott 55% — transferable currency is worth more left in place than parked in a hotel program you may not redeem.
