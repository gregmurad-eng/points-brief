Lead Story · The Hyatt Crown Slips for Sapphire Preferred

The best-kept secret in the points world has quietly become a little less golden. Chase confirmed that the Sapphire Preferred and Ink Business Preferred will lose their cherished 1:1 transfer ratio to World of Hyatt, dropping to 4:3 — a 25% haircut. Every 1,000 Ultimate Rewards points that once became 1,000 Hyatt points will soon yield just 750.

The timing is split, and it matters. Anyone who applies on or after June 15 — tomorrow — inherits the new ratio immediately. Existing Sapphire Preferred holders keep the 1:1 rate until October 1, 2026, a four-month runway to move points or reconsider where the card sits in the wallet. Crucially, the 1:1 ratio survives untouched for Sapphire Reserve and Reserve for Business cardholders, sharpening the line between Chase's mid-tier and premium products.

Chase softened the blow with genuine upgrades: 3X on gas and vacation rentals, a doubled hotel credit, new emergency evacuation coverage, and no increase to the $95 annual fee. For travelers who lean on Hyatt—where a 1:1 transfer routinely buys outsized value at properties like the Park Hyatt — the calculus has nonetheless changed. If you carry the Preferred and bank on Hyatt, the message is plain: your premium-Hyatt pipeline now has a clock on it.

Today's Briefs

Chase → Marriott hits 55% — the richest hotel bonus of the season

Through June 30, 1,000 Ultimate Rewards points convert to 1,550 Bonvoy points. It is the strongest Chase-to-Marriott bonus on record this year, but the math only works if you have a specific high-value Marriott night in mind — Bonvoy's variable pricing dilutes blind speculative transfers.

Source: AwardWallet

United standardizes the 2026 earning cut

From April 2, 2026, standard MileagePlus members earn up to 40% fewer miles per flight and earn nothing on basic economy. The offset: expanded Polaris saver availability for cardholders and elites — a Premier-member cardholder can now find Polaris awards from 68,000 miles.

Source: AwardWallet

Amex doubles up to Marriott and Flying Blue

Membership Rewards carries two live bonuses through June 30: 25% to Air France-KLM Flying Blue and 20% to Marriott Bonvoy. Flying Blue's monthly Promo Rewards make the 25% the more interesting of the pair for transatlantic flyers.

Source: Upgraded Points

Delta trims its partner roster

Hawaiian Airlines exited the SkyMiles partner network as of mid-2025, with existing Hawaiian award tickets needing to be flown by April 1, 2026. Medallion MQD thresholds, at least, hold steady into 2026.

Source: Business Traveller
Today's Move

If Hyatt is your endgame and you hold the Sapphire Preferred, decide before October 1. You still transfer at 1:1 until then. Don't dump points speculatively — Hyatt points don't expire with activity and Marriott's 55% bonus is the louder near-term play. But if you have a Park Hyatt or Alila stay you know you'll book, locking those points into Hyatt while the ratio is still 1:1 is the cleanest value preservation available this week. Confirm the live ratio before you hit transfer.
