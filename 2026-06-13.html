Lead Story

Chase Sapphire Preferred Gets a Refresh — and a Hyatt Haircut

Chase rolled out a refresh of its flagship mid-tier card this week, and it cuts both ways. On the upside: new 3X categories on gas and vacation rentals, a doubled hotel credit, fresh emergency evacuation coverage, and — notably — no increase to the $95 annual fee. The catch is buried below the fold. Transfers to World of Hyatt, long the crown jewel of the Ultimate Rewards program, are being devalued from 1:1 to 4:3. Move 40,000 points and you will land just 30,000 Hyatt points — a 25% haircut on the single most valuable redemption partner Chase has.

Timing matters. New applicants on or after June 15 get the worse ratio immediately; existing Preferred cardholders are insulated until October 1. The 1:1 ratio survives untouched on the Sapphire Reserve, so Chase is effectively nudging its most Hyatt-hungry customers up the annual-fee ladder. For Greg's stack, the move is simple: if a Preferred application was on the table, it is now a this-weekend decision.

Today's Briefs

Hotels

Hyatt's new five-tier chart is now live

The award chart changes that went into effect May 20 are settling in. Hyatt moved from three pricing levels to five (Lowest, Low, Moderate, Upper, Top); 112 hotels jumped to a higher category against just 24 that dropped, and average award prices rose roughly 7.8%. Combined with the Chase transfer cut, Hyatt value is getting squeezed from both ends — worth re-pricing any aspirational redemption before you commit points. Details.

Transfers

Chase to Marriott at 55% is the headline rate of the month

Running May 16 through June 30, the 55% bonus turns 1,000 Ultimate Rewards points into 1,550 Bonvoy. Marriott points are not the strongest currency point-for-point, but at this ratio they become genuinely useful for high-end properties and the fifth-night-free on award stays. More.

Airlines

Amex to Flying Blue at 25% — the sleeper of the bunch

Flying Blue routinely runs Promo Rewards discounts to Europe, and stacking a 25% transfer bonus on top can produce some of the best transatlantic economy and business pricing available from a US transferable currency. Bonus ends June 30. Check current Promo Rewards before transferring speculatively.

Today's Move

If a Sapphire Preferred is anywhere on your radar, apply before June 15 to grandfather the 1:1 Hyatt ratio. Otherwise, sit tight on speculative Chase-to-Hyatt transfers — there is no bonus pushing them, and the chart just got more expensive. The cleanest value on the board this week is Amex to Flying Blue at 25% for a confirmed Europe itinerary. Transfer only against a redemption you can actually book.
