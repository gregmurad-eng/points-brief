Lead Story

The new Sapphire Preferred lands today — richer credits, a quietly poorer points engine

As of this morning, Chase has rolled out its long-teased overhaul of the Sapphire Preferred, and it is a study in give-and-take. The annual hotel credit doubles from $50 to $100, a complimentary year of Apple TV joins the lineup, and a Global Entry / TSA PreCheck / NEXUS credit of up to $120 every four years finally arrives — all while the annual fee holds at $95.

The catch sits in the fine print. The card's 10% anniversary points bonus is being retired, and there are quieter category and transfer-partner tweaks beneath the headline perks. Timing matters: anyone applying on or after today inherits the devaluations immediately, while existing cardholders are shielded until October 1, 2026. If you have been sitting on the fence, there is a real argument for applying before the negative changes bite — or for simply banking the next few months of the old earn structure if you already hold the card.

Today's Briefs

Hyatt's summer stack: 3,000 bonus points per long stay

Through July 31, World of Hyatt is handing out 3,000 bonus points on every stay of three or more consecutive nights — up to 30,000 points across the promotion. Stackable with the separate 500-points-per-night offer at newly opened properties through June 30.

United's hotel-to-air bridge pays a 25% premium

Until June 30, transfers from eight hotel programs — Hyatt, Marriott, IHG, Hilton and more — into United MileagePlus earn a 25% bonus, capped at 25,000 bonus miles. Useful for topping off a MileagePlus balance, though hotel-to-airline ratios remain unkind for most.

IHG leans into the outdoors with Under Canvas

Through July 1, all 14 Under Canvas glamping properties earn 2,000 IHG One Rewards bonus points per night, with a separate 20-points-per-dollar offer for those who registered by June 12.

The Amex Platinum's $895 era

Amex's refreshed Platinum piles on up to $600 in annual hotel credits and premium hotel elite status — but the annual fee climbs to $895 from $695. Whether the coupon book outruns the fee depends entirely on how you travel.

Today's Move

The signal

If you have Marriott redemptions in your future, the Chase → Marriott 55% bonus is the standout play on the board right now — better than the Amex side's 20%, and live through June 30. Everything else this week is a "nice if it fits" rather than a "act now," with the lone exception of the Hyatt → Etihad bonus dying tonight.
