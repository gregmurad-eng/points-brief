Lead Story

Chase devalues Hyatt transfers — but existing cardholders get a four-month grace period

The single biggest move in your stack this month: Chase confirmed that the Sapphire Preferred and Ink Business Preferred will transfer to World of Hyatt at a 4:3 ratio instead of the long-standing 1:1, effective for anyone approved on or after June 15. If you already hold either card, you keep 1:1 transfers until October 1, 2026 — so your existing pipeline of Chase points to Hyatt is safe for now.

This matters because Hyatt has been the crown jewel of the Ultimate Rewards program, routinely returning outsized value on award nights. A 4:3 ratio quietly erases roughly a quarter of that edge for new applicants. The takeaway for you: there’s no rush, but Hyatt redemptions sourced from Chase points are now on a clock, and the smart play is to keep moving points only as you need them rather than speculatively. Read the full breakdown at NerdWallet and AwardWallet.

Today’s Briefs

Hyatt’s summer stay bonus is live

Register and earn 3,000 bonus points on every stay of three or more consecutive nights between June 1 and July 31, up to 30,000 total. Easy incremental value on stays you’re already booking. Details at AwardWallet.

Marriott points sale — 40%+ bonus through June 23

Marriott is selling Bonvoy points at a 40% or higher bonus with raised purchase limits until June 23. Only worth it against a specific high-value redemption, but the math can work for aspirational stays. Via LoyaltyLobby.

Hyatt points valuation slips to 1.55 cents

TPG’s June valuations marked Hyatt down from 1.65 to 1.55 cents per point — still the strongest hotel currency in your stack, but a reminder the gap is narrowing. See TPG valuations.

Sapphire Preferred refresh hits June 15

Alongside the Hyatt ratio change, the Sapphire Preferred adds perks at the same $95 fee: the annual Chase Travel hotel credit doubles to $100, plus new 3x categories on gas and vacation rentals. More at The Points Guy.
