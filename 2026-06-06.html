WINDOW CLOSING: The marquee June transfer bonuses — Chase→Marriott at 55% and both Amex→Marriott (20%) and Amex→Flying Blue (25%) — all expire June 30. Marriott’s buy-points bonus ends sooner, June 23. Chase→Southwest’s 30% bonus lapsed June 5.

Lead Story

Chase’s 55% Marriott bonus is the headline of the month

Through June 30, Chase Ultimate Rewards is transferring to Marriott Bonvoy at a 55% bonus — 1,000 Chase points become 1,550 Bonvoy points. That’s one of the richest Chase→Marriott ratios on record and, notably, it stacks against your existing Bonvoy balance for high-end redemptions like the Ritz-Carlton and St. Regis tier (after the 2026 dynamic adjustments).

The math only works if you have a specific award in mind. Marriott points run roughly 0.7–0.8 cents each, so even boosted you’re converting flexible Chase points (worth ~2 cents to a savvy redeemer) into a hotel currency worth less than half that. Transfer to book, not to speculate. See The Points Guy’s running bonus tracker and AwardWallet’s breakdown for the fine print.

Today’s Briefs

Hyatt opens a 12-month award booking window

Starting June 30, World of Hyatt Globalists, Explorists, and co-brand cardholders can book award nights up to 12 months out — a meaningful edge for hard-to-get Globalist-favorite properties. Details via TPG.

Hyatt summer promo: up to 30,000 bonus points

Register and earn 3,000 bonus points on each stay of three-plus consecutive nights, June 1–July 31, capped at 30,000. Skews toward Hyatt Place and Hyatt Select brands across the Americas. Terms at AwardWallet.

Marriott buy-points bonus ends June 23

Marriott is selling points with a 40%+ bonus and a raised annual cap (150,000). At an effective ~0.7¢/point it’s only worth it to top off for a specific premium booking, not to stockpile. Via LoyaltyLobby.

United sweetens PlusPoints earning for top tiers

Premier 1K and Global Services members now earn additional PlusPoints from PQP generated via eligible United card spend, with no cap. Upgrade awards also opened to all Premier members on award tickets as of February. Rundown at Upgraded Points.

Today’s Move

The Signal

If you have a concrete Marriott redemption on the calendar — a summer or fall premium-property stay — the Chase 55% is the single best action this month; transfer only the exact points the award needs before June 30. Otherwise, sit on your flexible balances. With no elevated airline transfer paths in your stack right now and AAdvantage holding its value edge (TPG June valuations), there’s no reason to lock points into a depreciating hotel currency on speculation.
