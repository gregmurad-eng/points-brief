Lead Story — The June 30 Cliff

Three transfer promotions that matter to your wallet expire at the end of the month, and the headline number is loud: Chase Ultimate Rewards is running a 55% bonus to Marriott Bonvoy through June 30, turning 1,000 Chase points into 1,550 Bonvoy. That is the richest Chase-to-Marriott ratio in recent memory.

The catch is the usual one. Marriott is among Chase's weakest transfer partners on a cents-per-point basis, so a 55% bump mostly closes the gap rather than creating an outright steal. The move only makes sense if you have a specific award stay in mind — a Category 6–8 property where the cash rate is ugly and the points math finally pencils out. Transfers are irreversible, so confirm availability before you push the button.

Running alongside it: Amex Membership Rewards is offering 20% to Marriott and 25% to Air France-KLM Flying Blue, both also ending June 30. Flying Blue is the more interesting of the two for transatlantic economy and the recurring Promo Rewards discounts. If you were already sitting on a plan, this is the weekend to execute.

Today's Briefs

Hyatt's award chart reset is now fully live

The first award-chart change in roughly five years took effect May 20, expanding from three tiers to five (Lowest, Low, Moderate, Upper, Top). 112 hotels moved up a category versus only 24 down, with some nights up to 67% pricier. Re-price any aspirational Hyatt redemption you'd been sitting on.

Early award access for Hyatt elites & cardholders

As of June 30, World of Hyatt Explorist/Globalist members and co-branded Hyatt cardholders get award space up to 13 months out — a real edge for peak-season Globalist bookings at constrained properties.

Chase tightens the Hyatt transfer ratio for new Sapphire Preferred

New Sapphire Preferred applicants on/after June 15 transfer to Hyatt at 4:3, not 1:1. Existing cardholders keep 1:1 through Sept 30, 2026. If you hold an older CSP, your Hyatt pipeline is intact — for now.

Valuations check

TPG's June 2026 valuations peg Chase UR at 2.05¢, Amex MR at 2.0¢, and Hyatt at 1.55¢ — context for whether any "55%" headline actually beats your alternatives.

Today's Move

If — and only if — you have a confirmed Category 6–8 Marriott stay in your cart, this is the weekend to transfer at the 55% Chase rate. No concrete booking? Don't speculatively move points into Bonvoy; let the bonus expire and keep your UR liquid. For everyone else, the standing Amex → Virgin Atlantic 30% (through July 31) is the lower-pressure value play worth eyeing for Delta-operated and ANA premium-cabin awards.
