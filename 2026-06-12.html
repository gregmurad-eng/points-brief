Lead Story

The Sapphire Preferred shake-up: a richer card, a poorer Hyatt pipeline

Chase has handed the Sapphire Preferred its biggest refresh in years, and the verdict for points travelers is genuinely split. On the earning side it's a clear win: starting June 15, the card adds 3x at gas and EV-charging stations and 3x on vacation rentals (Airbnb, Vrbo, Vacasa and friends), the annual Chase Travel hotel credit doubles from $50 to $100, and a Global Entry/TSA PreCheck credit joins the lineup. The annual fee, remarkably, doesn't move a dollar.

But the headline for transfer junkies is the cut nobody wanted: the World of Hyatt transfer ratio drops from 1:1 to 4:3. Forty thousand Ultimate Rewards points will soon buy just 30,000 Hyatt points. New applicants from June 15 feel it immediately; existing Sapphire Preferred holders are shielded until October 1, 2026. Crucially, Sapphire Reserve keeps the 1:1 ratio — so if Hyatt is core to your strategy, the Reserve just quietly became the more valuable card to hold UR-transferring points on.

Pair that with Hyatt's own award-chart devaluation that went live May 20 — 112 hotels moved up a category, only 24 down, and TPG's valuation of a Hyatt point slipped to 1.55 cents — and the message is consistent: the Hyatt sweet spot is narrowing on both ends. Lock in aspirational Hyatt redemptions while your 1:1 window holds.

Hyatt's five-tier chart is now the law of the land

The new structure (Lowest / Low / Moderate / Upper / Top) replaced the old off-peak/standard/peak system on May 20. Top-tier standard nights that were 40,000 points can now run 55,000, and peak dates at those properties reach as high as 75,000. US properties bore the brunt — 53% of the re-categorized hotels. Today's Move

If you hold a Sapphire Preferred, transfer Hyatt points before October 1.

Your 1:1 UR → Hyatt ratio is grandfathered only until Oct 1, 2026. If you have a high-value Hyatt redemption in mind — a Park Hyatt, an Alila, a Miraval — model it now and transfer in (Hyatt points don't expire as long as your account stays active within 24 months). After October, the same award costs you ~33% more UR. And if Hyatt is central to your travel, weigh moving your transferable balance onto a Sapphire Reserve, which keeps 1:1 indefinitely.

Secondary move: if a Marriott stay is on the calendar, the Chase → Bonvoy 55% bonus through June 30 is the richest hotel transfer of the year. Transfer only what a confirmed booking needs.
