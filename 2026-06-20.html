Lead Story

Chase's 55% Marriott bonus is the best transfer of the month — and the clock runs out June 30

For the back half of June, the most valuable button in your loyalty arsenal sits inside the Chase Ultimate Rewards transfer page. Through June 30, Chase is running a 55% transfer bonus to Marriott Bonvoy, turning 1,000 Ultimate Rewards points into 1,550 Bonvoy points. That is the richest Chase-to-Marriott ratio we've seen in well over a year, and it stacks neatly on top of Bonvoy's fifth-night-free award benefit.

The math gets interesting at the top of the chart. A 100,000-point Chase transfer lands as 155,000 Bonvoy points — enough, after the fifth-night-free credit, to cover a four-night-equivalent stay at many Category 6 and 7 properties. The caveat is the same as always with Marriott: confirm the cash-versus-points value on your specific dates before you pull the trigger, because Bonvoy's dynamic pricing can erase the edge on peak weekends. But as a way to top off a Bonvoy balance for a summer redemption you've already scoped, this is the move of the month.

Read the terms before transferring — points moved to a hotel program can't be moved back. Full details at AwardWallet.

Today's Briefs

◆ Chase–Hyatt ratio change now in effect for new cardholders

The Sapphire Preferred and Ink Business Preferred now transfer to World of Hyatt at 4:3 instead of 1:1 for anyone approved on or after June 15. Existing cardholders keep the 1:1 rate through September 30, then move to 4:3 on October 1. If Hyatt is central to your strategy, this is the strongest argument yet to keep a Sapphire Reserve or Ink Preferred opened before the cutoff — and to move any speculative Hyatt transfers over the summer rather than waiting. More at The Points Guy.

◆ United quietly raises the price of close-in awards

United has nudged up the cost of close-in MileagePlus awards again, with some last-minute redemptions now running roughly a third higher than before. It's the latest in a run of dynamic-pricing creep across the big three. The practical takeaway: book MileagePlus awards earlier when you can, and price the same itinerary in partner currencies before committing. Background at Live and Let's Fly.

◆ Flying Blue is the sleeper play on the Amex menu

With Amex running 25% to Flying Blue through month-end, transatlantic Air France and KLM economy and business awards are worth a fresh look — Flying Blue's monthly Promo Rewards routinely discount specific routes by up to a third. Pair the transfer bonus with a discounted Promo Reward and the effective cost can drop sharply. Check the Promo Rewards list for your origin before transferring. Details at AwardWallet.

◆ The valuations backdrop: AAdvantage still leads

In the latest monthly valuations, AAdvantage miles continue to grade out near the top of the U.S. legacy programs on the strength of partner award space, while Delta SkyMiles remain anchored to cash-like pricing. Nothing here is an emergency — but it's a useful reminder to spend the weakest currency first and hoard the flexible points that can chase whichever program is running a bonus. TPG June valuations.

Today's Move

If a Marriott summer stay is already on your calendar, top off the balance now.

The Chase 55% bonus and the Amex 20% bonus both close June 30. If you have a confirmed Bonvoy redemption in mind, calculate the exact points shortfall, transfer just enough to cover it (plus a small cushion), and lock the award. Don't speculatively dump points into Bonvoy for a stay you haven't priced — hotel points can't come back, and dynamic pricing can move under you. Targeted top-offs only.
